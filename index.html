Call For Proposals!

From mass surveillance to the over-commercialization of the Internet, the concept of a free and open Internet is being threatened by corporate interests and over-reaching governments. Radical Networks is an environment for critical discussion around these issues and a place to learn more about policy, DIY networking and how computer networks work.

We invite artists, lawyers, engineers, writers, policy makers, educators, scientists, journalists, community organizers, and more to present your ideas in this yearâ€™s Radical Networks. Submit a proposal for a workshop, a talk, a performance or to participate in our exhibition. Some possible topics include:

  • Offline networks
  • Mesh networks
  • Local networks for community
  • Experimental application of computer networks
  • Networks for political activism
  • Network security for ordinary citizens
  • Artistic use of computer networks
  • Personal networks
  • Bringing connectivity to rural areas
  • Experimental social networks
  • Control and ownership of networks
  • Ethical hacking
  • ...and anything else that takes a fresh or critical look at how networks are used.

Submissions are due by Friday June 30, 2017 at 11:59pm.

To submit your proposal, follow the instructions here.

The call for participation is open to all! We encourage women, people of color, LGBTSTGNC, immigrants, and differently-abled folks to apply!
